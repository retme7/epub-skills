

# *The Last Lantern*

The town had forgotten how to speak in daylight. Houses leaned toward each other like old friends sharing secrets, their windows dim with dust and silence.

Clara walked the cobbled streets with a lantern that never seemed to burn out. She lit it not for herself, but for the stories that had no mouths to tell them. Each flicker revealed fragments: a child’s toy lying half-buried in dirt, a letter yellowed with time, a pair of shoes by the doorway that would never be worn again.

At the edge of the town, the river ran backward, carrying echoes of laughter and sorrow in equal measure. She leaned over, letting the lantern’s reflection ripple across the water. For a moment, the town breathed again, its memory stretching like the river, restless but alive.

And then the wind came—a soft, insistent murmur—and the lantern swung, spilling light onto the empty streets. Clara smiled, knowing that some stories never die; they only wait for someone to remember them.
